> **Binding cancer-domain guardrails (read first — they gate everything below).**
> This project produces teaching datasets for **cancer-data education**. It may use **only**
> open-access / aggregate / **de-identified** or **synthetic** data. **Controlled-access data
> (dbGaP, EGA, individual-level biobanks) and any identifiable patient data are OUT OF SCOPE** —
> they require authorized access + IRB this project will not seek. Every source license is
> verified before use. The outputs are **education-only**: every artifact carries a **"not medical
> advice / not for clinical use / not real patients"** notice, and every factual/clinical/
> statistical assertion (including every parameter a synthetic generator is calibrated to) carries
> **provenance**. Any patient-facing educational narrative or clinical-decision content is escalated
> to **`riskTier: high`** and requires **oncologist + patient-advocate sign-off** — and is largely
> kept out of scope (the audience is *data learners*, not patients). For any registry-shaped data:
> consent-first, **deceased/aggregate only**, schema-as-governance, never a live patient data store.

## Executive summary

Learning to work with cancer data — survival analysis, differential expression, variant
annotation, staging logic, epidemiology, messy-data cleaning — is gatekept by a data problem.
The realistic datasets are **controlled-access** (dbGaP, EGA, individual-level biobanks) and take
months of IRB/DAC approval a student or self-learner cannot obtain; the freely-downloadable ones
are either toy tables with no cancer context, or large real archives whose license, provenance,
and de-identification status are murky and whose size makes them unusable in a 90-minute lesson.
The result: people who want to learn cancer data science practice on iris and titanic, then meet
real oncology data cold.

This project produces **small, rigorously-documented, openly-licensed teaching datasets** for
cancer-data education, plus the **exercises, solutions, and notebooks** that turn each dataset
into a lesson. Two production modes, both safe by construction:

1. **Synthetic** (the default and majority). Datasets generated by a documented, reproducible
   generative model (fixed seed, published parameters) calibrated to **published *aggregate*
   statistics** — e.g. a stage distribution or a Kaplan–Meier curve from a paper — never to any
   individual-level record. The data are clinically *plausible* but contain **no real patients**,
   so re-identification is impossible by construction.
2. **Derived-from-open** (the minority, gated hard). Small subsets/aggregations of data that are
   *already* open-access **and** already de-identified or aggregate (e.g. published SEER/GLOBOCAN
   aggregate tables, open-tier expression matrices), used only after per-source license + de-id
   verification, with attribution and a propagated-compatible license.

The **deliverable is the teaching dataset + its documentation + its exercises**, not a research
dataset and not medical content. The hard constraints — front-loaded as blocking gates — are the
cancer-domain guardrails above: source eligibility, de-identification, license, the synthetic-only
default, and the "not real patients / not medical advice / education-only" framing on every file.

Risk tier is **low** because the dominant mode is synthetic data for a data-science audience.
The risks are not technical but **factual, legal, and ethical**: implying synthetic data is real;
mis-stating a source license; deriving from something that should have been out of scope; or
letting a teaching exercise drift into clinical interpretation. The plan makes each of those a
non-skippable gate, with named escalation to medium (license/de-id judgement) and high (any
patient-facing clinical content — kept out of scope).

## Problem & beneficiaries

**Who is helped.**
- **Learners** — bioinformatics/biostatistics students, early-career analysts, clinicians learning
  data skills, and **self-taught learners** with no institutional data access. They are the primary
  beneficiaries: they need realistic-but-safe cancer data to practice on.
- **Educators & course authors** — instructors building cancer-data courses, workshop leaders
  (e.g. Carpentries-style), MOOC authors, and TA teams who currently improvise or use non-oncology
  toy data. A documented, license-clean, drop-in dataset + exercise saves them the legal/curation
  work and the de-identification risk.
- **Patient advocates & science communicators** learning to *read* cancer data and statistics so
  they can engage with research on behalf of patients (education-only; not clinical training).
- **The open-science / reproducibility commons** — small, citable, fully-provenanced datasets are
  reusable as method-development and CI test fixtures far beyond a single classroom.

**The verified need.** The *general* need — safe, realistic, well-documented open cancer teaching
data — is well established (controlled-access barriers and "toy data" gaps are widely cited in
bioinformatics-education literature). We treat the general need as real but the **per-partner,
per-course need as TO BE SECURED**: no named course, workshop, or training program has yet agreed
to adopt these datasets. Until a specific educator/program confirms they will use a dataset in
real teaching, individual tasks carry **`verifiedNeed: false`**. This honesty matters: "delivered,
not merged" requires a dataset to be *adopted into real teaching* (or demonstrably reused), not
merely produced and posted.

**Partner org.** TO BE SECURED. Candidate adoption channels: Data Carpentry / The Carpentries
(genomics & ecology lesson model), Bioconductor / Galaxy Training Network, a university
bioinformatics or biostatistics course, a cancer-data MOOC, and patient-advocate research-literacy
programs. M0 includes explicit adoption-channel outreach; no partner is assumed.

## Goals and non-goals

**Goals**
- Produce a **reusable teaching-dataset template + canonical metadata/datasheet model** so every
  dataset ships with identical, complete documentation (datasheet, data dictionary, provenance,
  license, generation recipe/seed, known limitations & biases, safety notice).
- Ship a **documented, reproducible synthetic-data generation methodology** whose every parameter
  is provenanced to a published aggregate source (provenance-on-every-assertion, made operational).
- Deliver a small, growing **library of teaching datasets**, each paired with **learning
  objectives, exercises, worked solutions, and runnable notebooks** (Python and, where it serves
  the audience, R), at graded difficulty.
- Make **source-eligibility + de-identification + license verification a blocking, auditable gate**
  that encodes the cancer guardrails (synthetic-default, no controlled-access, no identifiable data).
- Achieve **real adoption**: datasets used in at least one real course/workshop and reusable as
  open, citable artifacts (Zenodo DOI).

**Non-goals**
- We do **not** touch **controlled-access** data (dbGaP, EGA, individual-level biobanks) or any
  identifiable patient data — ever, under any mode.
- We do **not** produce **medical advice, clinical-decision content, or patient-facing treatment
  guidance**. Datasets are for *data-skills* teaching; any clinical narrative is out of scope
  (and would be `riskTier: high`, oncologist+advocate-gated, which this project does not staff).
- We do **not** claim synthetic data is real, nor present any teaching dataset as suitable for
  research conclusions, model validation for clinical use, or epidemiological inference.
- We do **not** re-identify, link, or re-aggregate de-identified data toward smaller cells; we do
  not lower a publisher's anonymization (no transforming toward re-identification).
- We do **not** build a registry or any live patient data store. Registry-shaped teaching artifacts
  are **schema/governance examples only**, populated with synthetic or deceased/aggregate data.
- We do **not** auto-publish; a human reviews and a human submits each release.

## Success metrics (outcomes)

Outcome-based and beneficiary-centric. Vanity metrics ("datasets produced", "downloads") are
explicitly **not** success on their own; adoption and learning are.

| Metric | Baseline | Target (first 6 months) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Teaching datasets **adopted into a real course/workshop/curriculum** (last-mile delivered, evidence recorded) | 0 | ≥ 4 datasets adopted in ≥ 2 distinct programs |
| Learners who **completed the paired exercises** (educator-reported or platform metric) | 0 | ≥ 100 learner-completions across adopted datasets |
| Confirmed **adoption partners** (educator/program committing to use a dataset) | 0 | ≥ 2 secured |
| **Re-identification / real-patient-leak incidents** | 0 | **0 (hard zero — any incident is a sev-1 stop-the-line)** |
| **Safety/medical-claim defects** found in review (synthetic-implied-real, license error, clinical claim) | n/a | **0** shipped; tracked per release |
| Datasets passing the source-eligibility + de-id + license gate / total triaged | n/a | 100% of shipped datasets have a recorded, verifiable license + de-id/synthetic provenance |
| **Reuse signal**: dataset cited / forked / reused by a third party outside the origin course | 0 | ≥ 3 verifiable external reuse events (citation, fork-with-use, course inclusion) |
| Every dataset is **reproducible from its recipe** (synthetic) or **re-derivable from source** (derived) by an independent reviewer | n/a | 100% (independent re-run reproduces the published artifact byte-for-byte for synthetic; cell-for-cell for derived) |

**On attribution of outcomes.** A "reuse" or "adoption" event must be **externally verifiable** —
a syllabus/workshop page listing the dataset, an educator's written confirmation, a citing
preprint/issue/PR, or a platform completion metric. Self-reported reuse does not count.

**Quantifying "well-documented" (so DoDs are checkable).** Each dataset carries a
**documentation-completeness score (0–100)**: fraction of canonical fields populated and
source-verified (datasheet sections answered, data dictionary covering every column, provenance
complete incl. generation seed/params or derivation steps, license recorded with `permitsReuse`/
`permitsDerivatives`, safety notice present, ≥ 1 exercise with solution, valid Croissant emitted).
**Target: every shipped dataset ≥ 90/100.** Synthetic datasets additionally require a
**reproducibility check = PASS** (independent re-run from the recorded seed/params reproduces the
file).

## Scope

**In scope**
- **Synthetic teaching datasets** (default): reproducible generation from published-aggregate
  parameters; clinical-tabular cohorts (survival/Kaplan–Meier/Cox practice), differential-expression
  matrices, variant/mutation tables, deliberately "messy" data-cleaning sets, staging-logic tables.
- **Derived-from-open teaching datasets** (gated): small subsets/aggregations of *already-open,
  already de-identified or aggregate* sources (e.g. published SEER/GLOBOCAN aggregate incidence,
  open-tier expression matrices) — only after the eligibility+de-id+license gate passes.
- **Teaching layer per dataset**: learning objectives, prerequisites, difficulty, exercises +
  worked solutions, runnable notebook(s) (Python; R where it serves the audience), and an
  instructor note.
- **Documentation per dataset**: Datasheet-for-Datasets, machine-readable data dictionary,
  provenance record (incl. generation recipe + seed or derivation steps), license record, known
  limitations & biases, and the mandatory safety/"not real patients / not medical advice" notice.
- **Light tooling**: reproducible generator harness, a validation/quality script, a Croissant ML
  metadata emitter, and exercise autograding scaffolding.
- **Packaging**: open formats (CSV/TSV + Parquet where helpful, JSON/JSON-LD metadata), versioned,
  with a Zenodo DOI per release.

**Out of scope**
- Controlled-access data and **all** identifiable patient data (no exceptions, no "lightly
  anonymized" real micro-data).
- Medical advice, clinical-decision support, diagnosis/prognosis tools, or treatment guidance.
- Real research datasets, benchmark leaderboards intended for clinical-grade model validation, or
  any claim of fitness for research/clinical inference.
- De-anonymization, record linkage, or any transformation that *reduces* a source's anonymization.
- A live registry / patient data store of any kind.
- Hosting or mirroring large upstream archives; auto-publishing without human review.

## Solution approach & architecture

This is a **content/data project with light, reproducible tooling** — a dataset *factory* with a
safety gate, not a data pipeline that moves real patient data.

**Per-dataset pipeline.**
1. **Learning-objective definition** — name the skill the dataset teaches (e.g. "fit and interpret
   a Cox model with censoring"), the audience, prerequisites, and difficulty. This drives design;
   we build datasets to teach a thing, not data in search of a lesson.
2. **Mode & source decision (the gate, blocking)** — choose **synthetic** (default) or
   **derived-from-open**. For derived, the source must be open-access **and** already
   de-identified/aggregate, with a verified, reuse+derivative-permitting license and **no
   controlled-access** lineage. Decision recorded as a committed PASS/FLAG/EXCLUDE artifact.
3. **Generation (synthetic) or curation (derived).**
   - *Synthetic*: a documented generative model with a **fixed RNG seed** and parameters each
     **provenanced to a published aggregate source** (citation + value). Output is deterministic
     and re-runnable. No individual-level data is ever an input.
   - *Derived*: bounded subset/aggregation under the inspection access protocol below; never
     re-identifying, never narrowing cells below the publisher's anonymization.
4. **Safety & plausibility review** — confirm: clinically *plausible* yet explicitly synthetic;
   "no real patients" + "not medical advice / not for clinical use" notice present; no individual
   is implied; aggregate cells respect k-anonymity (k ≥ 5) for any derived data.
5. **Documentation** — datasheet, data dictionary, provenance (recipe+seed or derivation),
   license record, known limitations & biases, safety notice.
6. **Teaching layer** — exercises + worked solutions + runnable notebook(s) + instructor note,
   with learning objectives and difficulty.
7. **Packaging & metadata** — open formats; canonical metadata object → Croissant ML + Zenodo
   record; semantic version; validation script + quality report.
8. **Review & publish** — technical reviewer (data + code + reproducibility), educator reviewer
   (pedagogy), and **domain reviewer** (clinical/biological plausibility + de-id adequacy) for any
   derived-from-real or clinically-flavoured dataset. A human submits the release; the steward
   records adoption.

**Canonical metadata model (single source of truth; all outputs are projections of it).**
`id`, `title`, `learningObjectives[]`, `audience`, `difficulty`, `prerequisites[]`,
`mode {synthetic|derived}`, `source {name, url, license{id,url,permitsReuse,permitsDerivatives,
shareAlike,snapshotRef}, accessTier:"open-access", deIdStatus, controlledAccessLineage:false}`,
`generation {seed, model, params[]{name,value,provenance{citation,url}}}` (synthetic) **or**
`derivation {steps[], aggregationLevel, kAnonymityMin}` (derived), `safety {syntheticLabel:true|…,
notMedicalAdvice:true, notForClinicalUse:true, realPatients:false}`, `fields[]{name,type,units,
allowedValues,nullable,description,caveats}`, `knownLimitations[]`, `biases[]`, `exercises[]{prompt,
solutionRef,difficulty}`, `formats[]`, `specVersions{croissant}`, `version`, `doi`,
`completenessScore{before,after}`, `reproducibilityCheck`.

**Synthetic generation harness.** A small, dependency-light TypeScript/Node (and/or documented
Python) generator that: takes a parameter file (each param provenanced), a fixed seed, and emits the
data file + a `provenance.json`. Determinism is a hard requirement — same seed + params ⇒ identical
bytes — so any reviewer can reproduce the dataset and confirm it was *not* sampled from real records.

**Dataset-inspection access protocol (for *derived* mode only — makes "open + de-identified only"
enforceable).** Inspection is the only point we touch a real source, so it is constrained:
- **Open-access sources only**; verify there is **no controlled-access lineage** before any read.
- **Header/aggregate/streamed reads**, bounded sample (≤ 1,000 rows or ≤ 5 MB) only to confirm
  schema and de-id status — never a full extract for republication.
- **Aggregate-or-coarser out.** Any derived release is at the publisher's aggregation level or
  coarser; never finer. Enforce **k ≥ 5** minimum cell size on any cross-tabulation we publish.
- **Stop on identifiability signal.** Any direct identifier, sub-k cell, or re-identifiable
  quasi-identifier combination ⇒ halt, discard sample, route to EXCLUDE.
- **No real rows committed.** Worked examples/exercises use synthetic or trivially-public rows.

**Tech stack.** TypeScript, ESM, pnpm workspaces (Elyos convention) for tooling; data in CSV/TSV/
Parquet; metadata in JSON/JSON-LD (Croissant ML v1.0, pinned). Notebooks in Jupyter (Python) and
R Markdown where the audience benefits. No runtime services; everything runs locally or in CI.

**Key decisions.**
- **Synthetic-by-default.** Derived-from-real is the exception and is gated; this keeps the bulk of
  the work firmly `low` risk and re-identification-proof by construction.
- **Provenance-on-every-parameter.** A synthetic dataset is only as trustworthy as the cited
  aggregate statistics it's calibrated to; every parameter records its source.
- **Determinism is a gate.** A synthetic dataset that cannot be reproduced byte-for-byte from its
  recorded seed/params fails review — reproducibility is also the proof it isn't real data.
- **Safety notice is non-removable** and embedded in every artifact (data header comment, datasheet,
  notebook first cell, and metadata).

## Data, licensing & compliance

**This is the critical section and it leads with the cancer-domain guardrails.** Even though the
default mode is synthetic, we treat sourcing, licensing, and identifiability conservatively because
(a) derived datasets touch real open data and (b) synthetic generators are *calibrated to* cited
sources whose licenses and aggregate nature must be respected.

**Cancer-domain eligibility gate (binding, blocking, applied before any generation/curation).**
- **Synthetic mode** — inputs are **only published aggregate statistics** (rates, distributions,
  summary curves) from the literature/authoritative reports, each cited. No individual-level data,
  ever, even as a "fitting" input. Output is labeled synthetic.
- **Derived mode** — source must be **open-access** (freely available, no DAC/IRB), **already
  de-identified or aggregate**, with **no controlled-access lineage**. Anything sourced from or
  derivable to **dbGaP, EGA, individual-level biobanks, or identifiable patient records is
  EXCLUDED outright** — not "anonymize and proceed."
- **Registry-shaped artifacts** — schema/governance examples only; if populated, **synthetic or
  deceased/aggregate** data only, consent-first framing; **never** a live patient store.

**Source licenses we accept (must permit reuse AND derivative works for *derived* mode; for
*synthetic* mode the same applies to any data table we calibrate from):**
- **CC0 1.0** — accepted; no attribution required (we still record provenance).
- **CC-BY 4.0** (and compatible versions) — accepted; attribution required and propagated.
- **US-government public domain** (e.g. SEER/NCI aggregate published tables, CDC) — accepted;
  provenance recorded.
- **Open Government Licence (OGL)** v2.0/v3.0 — accepted; attribution required.
- **Share-alike (CC-BY-SA, ODbL)** and **non-commercial (CC-BY-NC, and notably COSMIC/OncoKB
  non-commercial research terms)** → governed by a written **NC/share-alike acceptance policy**
  (`otd-policy-…`) decided **before** any triage. **Default for NC cancer resources is EXCLUDE**
  (their terms typically forbid the open redistribution a teaching commons requires); share-alike
  sources, if accepted, **propagate** their license to our output.
- **No clear license / "all rights reserved" / forbids derivatives** → **FLAG and EXCLUDE**, never
  guessed.

**Objective acceptance criterion.** A source PASSes only if its license is on the accepted list (or
accepted by the NC/share-alike policy) **and** an explicit `license.permitsReuse: true` (and
`permitsDerivatives: true` for derived/subsetting) is recorded with a **cited clause/URL**, **and**
the cancer-eligibility gate passes (open-access, de-identified/aggregate/synthetic, no
controlled-access lineage). Missing evidence = FLAG/EXCLUDE, never default-allow.

**Provenance model.** Every dataset records: mode (synthetic/derived); for synthetic — the
generator, the **fixed seed**, and **every parameter with its citation + value**; for derived —
source URL, publisher, retrieval timestamp, version/edition, derivation steps, aggregation level,
and k-anonymity minimum; plus license id + URL + a **captured license-text snapshot** (committed
copy + SHA-256 + Wayback save URL), required attribution string, and update cadence. Provenance is
part of the committed deliverable. **Provenance-on-every-assertion** is enforced: any clinical or
statistical claim in a datasheet/exercise cites its source.

**Identifiability / PII stance.** By construction, synthetic data has no PII. For derived data,
identifiability triage is mandatory and blocking, using the same methodology as the inspection
protocol: direct-identifier column heuristics; quasi-identifier **k-anonymity (k ≥ 5)** on any
published cross-tab; geo-precision threshold; linkage risk. Any flag ⇒ EXCLUDE (we never anonymize
real data ourselves — that is out of scope). We never publish cells finer than the source.

**Attribution & output licensing.**
- **Synthetic datasets + their documentation/exercises** → **CC0 1.0** (maximize teaching reuse;
  no real source to attribute), *unless* calibrated from a CC-BY aggregate whose attribution we
  then carry → then **CC-BY-4.0** with the cited sources attributed.
- **Derived datasets** → license **compatible with and propagated from** the source (CC-BY → CC-BY
  4.0 with attribution; share-alike → the share-alike license). We never relicense to something
  more permissive than the source allows.
- **Code (generator/validator/Croissant emitter/autograder)** → **MIT**.
- Every artifact attributes calibration/derivation sources, links them, and clearly states the
  dataset is **synthetic (or derived from open aggregate data) for education only — not real
  patients, not medical advice, not for clinical use**.

## Quality, review & risk gates

**Risk tier: `low`** for the default work (synthetic datasets + exercises for a data-science
audience), with **explicit, documented escalation**:
- **→ `medium`** when license/de-id judgement is involved (any *derived-from-real* dataset, any
  ambiguous/NC/share-alike license, any aggregate cell-size judgement).
- **→ `high`** for any **patient-facing clinical/medical narrative or decision content** —
  requires **oncologist + patient-advocate sign-off** and is **kept out of scope** here (the
  audience is data learners). If a task is found to require clinical interpretation, it is split out
  and re-tiered rather than shipped under this project.

**Required review before a dataset is "done":**
- **Technical reviewer (mandatory)** — verifies data dictionary, formats, Croissant metadata, the
  validation script (CI green), and the **reproducibility check** (independent re-run from
  seed/params reproduces a synthetic dataset; re-derivation reproduces a derived one).
- **Safety / eligibility reviewer (mandatory)** — confirms the cancer-eligibility gate passed
  (open-access, de-identified/aggregate/synthetic, no controlled-access lineage), the license is
  recorded and permits the chosen output license, and the **"synthetic / not real patients / not
  medical advice / not for clinical use" notice is present and accurate**. This is the hard gate;
  it must be filled before the M0 pilot is reviewed.
- **Educator reviewer (mandatory for the teaching layer)** — confirms learning objectives,
  exercises, solutions, and notebooks actually teach the stated skill at the stated level.
- **Domain reviewer (oncologist/bioinformatician)** — required for any **derived-from-real** or
  clinically-flavoured dataset, to confirm clinical/biological **plausibility** and de-id adequacy.
  Escalates the task to `riskTier: medium`. (A `high`-tier clinical narrative would additionally
  require advocate sign-off — but such content is out of scope.)

**Test fixtures & golden files (so "CI green" means something).** Each tool ships committed test
assets exercised in CI, using only synthetic/public fixtures: a Croissant validator with
known-valid + deliberately-malformed golden JSON-LD; generator determinism tests (same seed ⇒
identical bytes); validation-script golden input→report pairs; and an autograder test that a
reference solution scores full marks and a known-wrong answer does not.

**Definition of Shipped.** A teaching dataset is *shipped* when: it passed the eligibility+de-id+
license gate (artifact committed); its documentation-completeness score ≥ 90/100; its
reproducibility check = PASS; it has ≥ 1 exercise with a worked solution and a runnable notebook;
the safety notice is present on every artifact; it has a Zenodo DOI; **and a beneficiary has adopted
it** — used in a real course/workshop (steward records the adoption-evidence artifact
`outcomes/<dataset-id>.json` with the syllabus/workshop URL or educator confirmation) — or, if no
channel has yet materialized, it is **published with the adoption blocker surfaced**. Producing the
dataset is not shipped; recorded adoption (or a verifiable external reuse event) is.

## Roadmap & milestones

**M0 — Foundation, safety gate & cold-start (thin)**
- Goal: build the reusable template + synthetic-generation methodology + the eligibility/de-id/
  license/safety gate; prove the end-to-end flow on one synthetic pilot dataset; begin adoption
  outreach.
- **Cold-start de-risking.** The pilot is **synthetic** (no source dependency, lowest risk) and a
  staple teaching need — a **synthetic survival-analysis cohort** (stage, treatment arm, censored
  survival times calibrated to *published aggregate* survival statistics). Adoption is de-risked by
  a **self-serve fallback**: even without a partner, we publish to **Zenodo (DOI)** and submit as a
  contributed lesson to an open training repo (GitHub PR), so a real *reusable* outcome exists.
- Exit criteria: (1) teaching-dataset template + canonical metadata model published; (2) synthetic
  generation methodology (seed + provenanced params + determinism requirement) documented; (3) the
  eligibility/de-id/license/safety gate checklist + NC/share-alike policy exist and are applied to
  the pilot; (4) Croissant validator + validation script green in CI with golden fixtures;
  (5) one synthetic pilot dataset fully produced end-to-end — gate PASS, ≥ 90/100 completeness,
  reproducibility PASS, ≥ 1 exercise+solution+notebook, Zenodo DOI, safety notice — and **adopted**
  via a confirmed channel or **published** via the self-serve fallback with the blocker surfaced;
  (6) safety/eligibility reviewer named; (7) ≥ 1 adoption-outreach thread opened.

**M1 — Source catalog, generation methodology hardened & first adoptions**
- Goal: make the gate + generation rigorous, broaden dataset types, secure first real adoption.
- Exit criteria: (1) a **candidate-source catalog** (open-access cancer aggregate/synthetic-input
  sources + target learning objectives), license-classified, committed; (2) ≥ 3 datasets shipped
  (gate artifact each), covering ≥ 2 skill areas (e.g. survival + differential-expression +
  data-cleaning); (3) ≥ 1 confirmed adoption partner (`verifiedNeed: true` for their datasets);
  (4) exercise/solution + autograding framework working in CI; (5) license-snapshot capture working
  in the decided format.

**M2 — Derived-from-open, epidemiology & scale**
- Goal: add the gated *derived-from-open* mode and aggregate-epidemiology datasets; scale the
  library; reduce per-dataset effort.
- Exit criteria: (1) ≥ 1 **derived-from-open** dataset shipped with full de-id/license artifact and
  domain-reviewer sign-off (riskTier medium); (2) ≥ 1 **aggregate incidence/epidemiology** teaching
  dataset (SEER/GLOBOCAN-style aggregate) shipped with k ≥ 5 respected; (3) ≥ 6 datasets shipped
  cumulatively across ≥ 3 skill areas; (4) Zenodo packaging + DOI + semantic versioning automated;
  (5) median per-dataset effort (author-session time + review cycles) measurably reduced vs. the
  M0/M1 baseline.

**M3 — Adoption outcomes, collection & sustainability**
- Goal: demonstrate real learning outcomes and a maintained, citable teaching collection.
- Exit criteria: (1) ≥ 4 datasets adopted across ≥ 2 programs with evidence artifacts; (2) ≥ 100
  learner-completions recorded; (3) ≥ 3 verifiable external reuse events; (4) a published
  **teaching collection / course pack** bundling the datasets with a unifying guide; (5) documented
  maintenance/versioning/errata process and a named steward for ongoing adoption liaison.

Dependencies: M1 depends on the M0 template + gate + generator; M2's derived mode depends on M1's
hardened gate and catalog; M3 depends on a body of adopted datasets from M1–M2.

## Security & privacy

- **Threat surface is small** (no runtime service, no real patient data hosted). Main surfaces are
  CI and the published artifacts.
- **The dominant privacy control is design, not defense:** synthetic-by-default means most datasets
  contain **no personal data at all**, and reproducibility-from-seed makes that auditable. Derived
  data is gated to open-access, already-de-identified/aggregate sources with k ≥ 5 enforced.
- **Secrets handling:** tooling needs no credentials by default. A Zenodo token (for DOI minting) is
  supplied by the human publishing and **must never** be written into logs, receipts, or committed
  files (Elyos rule). No API keys for any patient-data source exist because such sources are out of
  scope.
- **Abuse/misuse prevention:** refuse and flag any task that would (a) launder controlled-access or
  identifiable data as "teaching data", (b) push synthetic data toward implied-real clinical use,
  (c) add medical-advice content, or (d) reduce a source's anonymization. Datasets remain
  education-only, synthetic-or-open, fully provenanced.

## Sustainability & maintenance

- **Post-delivery ownership:** the steward maintains adoption relationships and records outcomes;
  the maintainer keeps the generator, validator, and Croissant emitter current with spec changes.
- **Versioning & errata:** semantic versions + Zenodo DOIs per release; an errata/regeneration
  process (synthetic datasets regenerate deterministically from the recorded seed/params, so fixes
  are reproducible). Stale/incorrect datasets become `maintenance` tasks.
- **Outcome tracking:** the steward records adoption events, learner-completions, and external reuse
  against the success metrics, reviewed each milestone. The reproducibility check is re-run on each
  release so every published dataset stays independently reproducible.

## Appendix A — Improvements applied

The following 25 specific improvements were identified during drafting and **applied** to the plan
above (and the companion `TASKS.md`). Each is concrete and reflected in the text, not aspirational.

1. **Synthetic-by-default architecture.** Made synthetic generation the primary mode (§Solution,
   §Scope), so the project is re-identification-proof by construction and stays `low` risk —
   rather than treating real-data de-identification as the main path.
2. **Cancer guardrails lead the data section.** §Data, licensing & compliance opens with the
   binding eligibility gate (open-access/de-id/synthetic-only, no controlled-access) before any
   license detail, per the binding domain rules.
3. **Explicit EXCLUDE list for cancer sources.** Named dbGaP, EGA, individual-level biobanks, and
   NC resources (COSMIC/OncoKB) as out-of-scope/EXCLUDE rather than leaving "controlled-access"
   abstract (§Data, §Dependencies, §Risks).
4. **Provenance-on-every-parameter, operationalized.** The canonical model's `generation.params[]`
   requires `{name,value,provenance{citation,url}}`, turning "provenance on every assertion" into a
   schema field a reviewer can check (§Solution).
5. **Determinism as a gate.** Same seed + params ⇒ identical bytes is a hard review criterion and
   doubles as proof the data isn't real (§Solution, §Quality, §Risks, M0 exit criteria).
6. **Non-removable safety notice.** "Synthetic / not real patients / not medical advice / not for
   clinical use" is required on *every* artifact (data header, datasheet, notebook first cell,
   metadata) and is a `safety{}` block in the canonical model (§Solution, §Data, §Quality).
7. **Documentation-completeness score (0–100) with ≥ 90 target.** Made "well-documented" a
   measurable DoD instead of a vibe (§Success metrics, §Quality).
8. **Reproducibility check added to Definition of Shipped.** Independent re-run must reproduce the
   artifact; added as a metric (100%) and a ship gate (§Success, §Quality).
9. **Outcome metrics are adoption- and learning-centric.** Datasets-adopted and learner-completions
   replace download/produced counts as the headline metrics (§Success).
10. **Hard-zero safety metrics.** Re-identification incidents and medical-claim defects are
    explicit **0 / sev-1 stop-the-line** targets (§Success, §Risks).
11. **Named risk-tier escalation rules.** Documented exactly when a task moves low→medium (license/
    de-id judgement) and medium→high (patient-facing clinical content, kept out of scope) (§Quality).
12. **Educator reviewer role added.** Pedagogy is reviewed separately from technical/safety, so
    datasets actually *teach* (§Solution, §Quality, §Governance).
13. **Domain (oncologist/bioinformatician) reviewer scoped to plausibility + de-id.** Required for
    derived/clinically-flavoured datasets, not a blanket high-tier gate (§Quality, §Governance).
14. **Dataset-inspection access protocol for derived mode.** Bounded sample, aggregate-or-coarser,
    k ≥ 5, halt-on-signal, no real rows committed — makes "open + de-identified only" enforceable
    (§Solution, §Data, §Security).
15. **k-anonymity (k ≥ 5) threshold made explicit** for any derived cross-tabulation we publish, and
    "never finer than the source" (§Data, §Security, M2 exit criteria).
16. **Output-license policy per mode.** CC0 for pure-synthetic, CC-BY when calibrated from CC-BY,

18. **License-text snapshot format fixed** (committed copy + SHA-256 + Wayback URL), mirroring the
